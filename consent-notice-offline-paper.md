## Context: The Paper Form Problem

Tens of thousands of Indian businesses collect personal data on paper. Application forms, KYC forms, worker registration packets, customer sign-up cards. In almost every case:

- There is no consent notice — the person filling the form is not told what the data will be used for
- The form does not name a Grievance Officer
- The form does not explain how to withdraw consent or request deletion
- Copies of the form are made, filed, shared with third parties, and never destroyed

The DPDP Act applies to paper-based data collection too. Section 5 requires a notice before consent — whether the consent is digital or handwritten.

This template gives you a printable consent notice to attach to your existing paper forms.

---

## Two Ways to Use This Template

**Option 1 — Separate notice sheet**
Print this notice as a separate page and attach it to the front of your existing form. The worker/customer reads it, then signs at the bottom of the notice before filling in the form.

**Option 2 — Integrated into your form**
Add a condensed version of this notice to the top of your existing form, above the signature box. This works better for short forms (1-2 pages).

### Your Rights

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you have the right to:

**Know and access** — You can ask us what information we hold about you. We will provide a copy within [TIMEFRAME — e.g., 7 working days].

**Correct** — If any information is wrong, contact us and we will correct it.

**Delete** — You can ask us to delete your information. We may not be able to delete information we are legally required to keep (such as PF or tax records), but we will delete everything else.

**Withdraw consent** — You can withdraw your consent for non-mandatory data at any time by contacting the Grievance Officer below.

## Implementation Notes

1. **Print in A4** — Use the `.html` version for a print-ready A4 format
2. **Translate** — Translate into the primary language of the people filling this form before printing
3. **Train your staff** — Anyone collecting forms must be able to explain this notice verbally to someone who cannot read
4. **File the signed notice separately** — Keep signed consent sheets separate from the data forms, in a secure location
5. **Number your forms** — Give each form a reference number so you can match it to the signed consent sheet if someone later requests deletion
6. **Digital backup** — Scan and store signed consent sheets digitally
