# DPDP Consent Notice Kit

**A free, open-source toolkit for Indian businesses to build DPDP-compliant consent notices, privacy policies, and consent withdrawal mechanisms.**

Built by [Specus Labs](https://github.com/specuslabs) · Licensed under CC BY 4.0

---

## What This Is

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) requires every Indian business that collects personal data to:

- Give users a clear **notice** before collecting their data
- Obtain **free, informed, specific, and unconditional consent**
- Provide a simple way for users to **withdraw consent**
- Honor withdrawal requests and notify any vendors who hold that data

Most Indian businesses are not doing any of this today — not because they don't want to, but because they don't know where to start.

This kit gives you the starting point. Ready-to-use templates, plain English guides, and sector-specific versions for D2C e-commerce, labour and staffing, and AI SaaS businesses.

---

## Who This Is For

- **D2C e-commerce founders** using Shopify, WooCommerce, or custom storefronts — your existing consent banners are almost certainly built for GDPR, not DPDP
- **Labour and staffing companies** collecting Aadhaar, PAN, bank details, and biometrics — paper forms with no consent notice are a significant compliance risk
- **Any Indian business** collecting personal data from customers, employees, vendors, or users
- **Founders and operators** — this kit is written for people running businesses, not for lawyers

## How to Use This Kit

1. **Start with** `USAGE-GUIDE.md` — it will tell you which files to use for your situation
2. **Read** the relevant section in `01-dpdp-reference/` to understand your obligations
3. **Pick your template** from `02-consent-notice-templates/` and `03-privacy-policy-templates/`
4. **Customise** — all templates use `[SQUARE BRACKET PLACEHOLDERS]` for fields you need to fill in
5. **Set up your withdrawal process** using `04-consent-withdrawal/`
6. **Review with a qualified legal professional** before going live
