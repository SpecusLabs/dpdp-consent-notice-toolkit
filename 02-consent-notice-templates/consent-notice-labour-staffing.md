## Context: Why This Sector Has Elevated Risk

Labour and staffing companies collect some of the most sensitive personal data in any sector:
- Aadhaar numbers
- PAN numbers
- Bank account details
- Photographs
- Biometrics (fingerprints, iris scans — in some operations)
- Caste certificates, disability certificates, and other sensitive identity documents

This data is collected at volume — sometimes hundreds or thousands of workers — often on paper forms, often by field staff with no training on data protection.

**The risk profile:**
1. **No consent notice** — workers sign a form but are not told what the data is used for, who it is shared with, or how to request deletion
2. **Data shared with client companies** — the company placing the workers receives copies of all this data
3. **No deletion capability** — if a worker asks for their data to be deleted, the staffing company cannot guarantee it has been deleted from all layers (paper files, digital records, client company systems)
4. **No Grievance Officer** — workers have no named person to contact with data concerns

This notice template addresses all four issues.

---

## A Note on Section 7 (Legitimate Use)

Much of the data collected during worker onboarding falls under **Section 7(e)** of the DPDP Act — employment-related processing does not require consent. This includes:
- PAN and Aadhaar for TDS, PF, and ESIC compliance
- Bank details for salary payment
- Biometrics for attendance (where legally required)

However, even where consent is not legally required, issuing a clear notice:
- Builds trust with workers
- Reduces grievance filings
- Protects you if the Data Protection Board investigates
- Is legally prudent given Rules are still being finalised

Use this notice for **all** data you collect — even data collected under legitimate use.

## Where to Use This Notice

- **Paper application form** — attach as page 1 (use the offline/paper version for print)
- **Digital onboarding form** — include before the form begins
- **Walk-in registration** — post visibly at your registration counter; give a printed copy to each worker
- **WhatsApp / app-based registration** — send as the first message before collecting any data

### Your Rights

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you have the right to:

- **Know** what data we hold about you and request a copy
- **Correct** any inaccurate information (for example, a wrong bank account number)
- **Erase** your data — subject to legal retention requirements (we must keep some records for 8 years under labour and tax law)
- **Withdraw** your consent for any data collected on the basis of consent (your work history, skills profile, etc.)
- **File a grievance** with our Grievance Officer
- **Complain** to the Data Protection Board of India

## Implementation Notes

1. **Print this notice** — most workers will register on paper. Use the `.html` version for a print-ready format.
2. **Translate it** — translate into the primary language of your workforce (Hindi, Marathi, Tamil, Bengali, etc.) before rolling out.
3. **Train your field staff** — whoever collects worker data must be able to explain this notice verbally to workers who cannot read.
4. **Log the consent** — keep a record of every signed consent form. Store it securely.
5. **Vendor notification** — when a worker withdraws consent, you must notify every client company who has their data. Use `04-consent-withdrawal/withdrawal-vendor-notification.md`.
